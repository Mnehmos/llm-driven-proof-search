## Problem Statement

### Setup

Let $f: \mathbb{R}^d \times \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}$ be twice continuously differentiable (not necessarily convex-concave), and consider the minimax problem

$
\min_{x \in \mathbb{R}^d}\; \max_{y \in \mathbb{R}^m} f(x,y).
$

Following the optimization order (min in $x$ then max in $y$ ), call $(x^*,y^*)$ a (possibly non-strict) local minimax point if there exists $r>0$ such that:

- 

 $y^*$ is a local maximizer of $y \mapsto f(x^*,y)$ (i.e., $\exists\,\delta>0$ such that $f(x^*,y^*) \ge f(x^*,y)$ for all $\|y-y^*\| \le \delta$ ); and

- 

 $x^*$ is a local minimizer of the localized upper-envelope $\phi_r(x) := \max_{\|y-y^*\| \le r} f(x,y)$ (i.e., $\exists\,\epsilon>0$ such that $\phi_r(x^*) \le \phi_r(x)$ for all $\|x-x^*\| \le \epsilon$ ). (Strict local minimax strengthens these inequalities to be strict for all nearby unequal points.)

### Unsolved Problem

Does there exist a first-order method $\mathcal{A}$ for general $C^2$ objectives (an algorithm that, at each iteration, may use past iterates, its internal randomness, and gradient queries $\nabla_x f(x_t,y_t)$ and $\nabla_y f(x_t,y_t)$ , but no higher-order information) such that for every $C^2$ function $f$ and every initialization distribution, with probability 1 every accumulation point (subsequence limit) of the iterates $(x_t,y_t)$ generated by $\mathcal{A}$ is a local minimax point (including non-strict local minimax points)? Equivalently, can one design a first-order method whose limiting behavior provably avoids convergence to local maximin points and other stationary points that are not local minimax points under this order-aware notion?

## Significance & Implications

A positive answer would provide an order-aware analogue of the informal guarantee sought in nonconvex minimization (that gradient-based dynamics converge only to local optima) for nonconvex-nonconcave minimax optimization. The COLT 2023 note specifically motivates this by GAN training: if mode collapse and other instabilities are driven by convergence to solutions aligned with maximin rather than the intended minimax objective, then a method that provably rules out non-minimax attractors would clarify what first-order information can guarantee and could guide the design of more stable minimax training algorithms. It would also sharpen which definition(s) of local minimax optimality are operationally meaningful for analyzing first-order dynamics in nonconvex-nonconcave games.

## Known Partial Results

- The COLT 2023 open-problem note asserts that existing gradient-based methods for nonconvex-nonconcave minimax problems do not (in theory or in practice) have the property that all their limit behavior is restricted to (local) minimax optima.

- Jin et al. proposed an order-aware notion of local minimax optimality for nonconvex-nonconcave minimax problems and gave a partial positive result: two-timescale gradient descent-ascent converges to strict local minimax optima.

- The same discussion highlights that convergence to general (non-strict) local minimax points has been left largely unexplored, despite the claim that non-strict local minimax points are prevalent.

- The COLT 2023 note reports recent findings that a two-timescale extragradient method can reach some non-strict local minimax points, providing partial evidence toward the broader goal.

- The note emphasizes that further progress likely depends on refining and agreeing on appropriate definition(s) of local minimax optimality in the nonconvex-nonconcave setting.
