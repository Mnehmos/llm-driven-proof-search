## Problem Statement

### Setup

Fix integers $k,C\ge 1$ , mixture weights $p_1,\dots,p_k>0$ with $\sum_{b=1}^k p_b=1$ , a class map $c:[k]\to [C]$ , and an aspect ratio $\phi\in(0,\infty)$ . For each $d$ , let class means $\mu_1,\dots,\mu_k\in\mathbb R^d$ , collect $\mu=(\mu_1,\dots,\mu_k)\in\mathbb R^{d\times k}$ , and let parameters $x=(x_1,\dots,x_C)\in\mathbb R^{d\times C}$ . For SNR parameter $\beta>0$ , data are generated by

$
B\sim\mathrm{Cat}(p_1,\dots,p_k),\qquad Y=\mu_B+\beta^{-1/2}\xi,\qquad \xi\sim N(0,I_d),
$

with observed class label $c(B)\in[C]$ . Define $q:=C+k$ and the summary Gram matrix

$
G=(x,\mu)^\top(x,\mu)\in\mathbb R^{q\times q}.
$

Call $G$ feasible if $G=(x,\mu)^\top(x,\mu)$ for some $(x,\mu)$ (equivalently, $G\succeq 0$ and realizable in some ambient dimension).

Fix a class index $\alpha\in[C]$ . Let $\mathrm{sm}_\alpha(u):=\exp(u_\alpha)/\sum_{j=1}^C\exp(u_j)$ for $u\in\mathbb R^C$ , and for $g\sim N(0,I_q)$ define

$
f_b^H(\beta^{-1/2}g;G):=\mathrm{sm}_\alpha\!\big(G_{[C],b}+G_{[C],[C]}^{1/2}\beta^{-1/2}g_{[C]}\big)\Big(1-\mathrm{sm}_\alpha\!\big(G_{[C],b}+G_{[C],[C]}^{1/2}\beta^{-1/2}g_{[C]}\big)\Big),
$

$
f_b^I(\beta^{-1/2}g;G):=\Big(\mathbf 1_{\{c(b)=\alpha\}}-\mathrm{sm}_\alpha\!\big(G_{[C],b}+G_{[C],[C]}^{1/2}\beta^{-1/2}g_{[C]}\big)\Big)^2.
$

For $M\in\{H,I\}$ , let $\nu_M(\beta,G)$ be the effective bulk law with Stieltjes transform $S_M$ solving

$
1+zS_M(z)=\phi\sum_{b=1}^k p_b\,\mathbb E\!\left[\frac{S_M(z)\,f_b^M(\beta^{-1/2}g;G)}{\beta\phi+S_M(z)\,f_b^M(\beta^{-1/2}g;G)}\right].
$

Define $F^M(z,G)\in\mathbb R^{q\times q}$ by

$
F^M_{ij}(z,G):=\beta\phi\sum_{b=1}^k p_b\,\mathbb E\!\left[\frac{f_b^M(\beta^{-1/2}g;G)}{\beta\phi+S_M(z)\,f_b^M(\beta^{-1/2}g;G)}\Big(\frac{g_i}{\sqrt\beta}+(\sqrt G)_{ib}\Big)\Big(\frac{g_j}{\sqrt\beta}+(\sqrt G)_{jb}\Big)\right].
$

Effective outliers are real roots of $\det(zI_q-F^M(z,G))=0$ ; define the right-outlier set

$
\mathcal O_M(\beta,G):=\{z\in\mathbb R:\det(zI_q-F^M(z,G))=0,\ z>\sup\operatorname{supp}(\nu_M(\beta,G))\},\qquad M\in\{H,I\}.
$

### Unsolved Problem

For fixed feasible $G$ , characterize the dependence of right-outlier existence on $\beta$ . In particular, determine whether

$
\beta_1\le \beta_2\ \Longrightarrow\ \mathbf 1\{\mathcal O_H(\beta_1,G)\neq\varnothing\}\le \mathbf 1\{\mathcal O_H(\beta_2,G)\neq\varnothing\},
$

and analogously with $\mathcal O_I$ in place of $\mathcal O_H$ . Also determine whether there exists $\beta_0(G)>0$ such that

$
0<\beta<\beta_0(G)\ \Longrightarrow\ \mathcal O_H(\beta,G)=\mathcal O_I(\beta,G)=\varnothing.
$

## Significance & Implications

Without this, the transition structure can be pathologically non-monotone, making global interpretation of SNR-dependent geometry unclear. A monotonicity/no-outlier-at-low-SNR theorem would complete the phase-transition narrative for this effective spectral framework. See [Arous et al. (2025)](#references) for details.

## Known Partial Results

The paper proves large- $\beta$ outlier existence but explicitly notes that a complementary small- $\beta$ no-outlier theorem is missing and that non-monotone scenarios are not ruled out. The problem is open as of January 22, 2026 (arXiv v3).
