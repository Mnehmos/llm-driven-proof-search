# IMO 2026 Problem 5

**Day 2 · Algebra (functional inequality)**

Let $\mathbb{R}_{>0}$ be the set of positive real numbers. Determine all
functions $f\colon \mathbb{R}_{>0} \to \mathbb{R}_{>0}$ such that

$$\sqrt{\frac{x^2 + f(y)^2}{2}} \;\ge\; \frac{f(x) + y}{2} \;\ge\; \sqrt{x f(y)}$$

for every $x, y \in \mathbb{R}_{>0}$.

---

Transcription notes:

- The outer terms are the quadratic and geometric means of the pair
  $\{x, f(y)\}$, but the middle term is the arithmetic mean of the *swapped*
  pair $\{f(x), y\}$ — the mixing across the two arguments is the content of
  the problem, not a typo.
